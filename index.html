Land of Civilizational Heritage

Central Java is a province located in the central part of the island of Java, Indonesia. It borders West Java to the west, East Java to the east, and the Indian Ocean and the Special Region of Yogyakarta to the south.

As the heartland of Javanese culture, this province holds an invaluable civilizational heritage — from the majestic Borobudur Temple to the world-renowned batik traditions of Solo. Every corner of this region tells a story of thousands of years of cultural splendor.

Known as a center of authentic art, craftsmanship, and cuisine, Central Java is a destination that blends a rich history with captivating natural beauty.

Cultural Richness

◆

Wayang Kulit (Shadow Puppetry)

A shadow puppet performance art recognized by UNESCO as an intangible cultural heritage. A dalang (puppeteer) performs hundreds of characters through an entire night, narrating the epics of the Mahabharata and Ramayana.

UNESCO Heritage All-Night Performance Javanese Philosophy

Solo & Pekalongan Batik

Two of the world's greatest batik centers. Solo batik is celebrated for its classic motifs and elegant sogan earth tones, while Pekalongan batik is renowned for its vibrant, dynamic coastal colors.

UNESCO Heritage Hand-Drawn Batik Stamped Batik

Gamelan

A traditional Javanese ensemble consisting of various percussion instruments. Central Javanese gamelan is distinguished by its soft, meditative sound, full of spiritual depth.

Gong Ageng Kendang Saron

Classical Javanese Dance

Bedhaya, Serimpi, and Golek dances are graceful court performances filled with symbolic meaning. Every movement carries a profound philosophy and spirituality unique to Javanese culture.

Local Cuisine

◆

Nasi Liwet Solo

Fragrant coconut-milk rice served with chicken opor, areh cream sauce, chayote, and braised egg. An iconic, legendary dish from the city of Solo.

From Surakarta

Es Dawet

A legendary Semarang snack filled with bamboo shoots, egg, and shrimp wrapped in a crispy thin skin. A harmonious blend of Chinese and Javanese culinary traditions.

From Semarang

Gudeg

Young jackfruit slow-cooked for hours in coconut milk and spices until it turns a rich reddish-brown. An authentic symbol of Central Javanese cuisine.

From Yogyakarta

Sate Blora

Tender goat satay from Blora, served with a signature peanut sauce alongside rice cakes and aromatic gulai broth.

From Blora98

Tempe Mendoan

A specialty from the Banyumas region of Central Java — battered tempeh fried using a unique technique. The word "mendo" in Javanese means "half-cooked" or "soft," giving this dish its distinctive tender texture.

From Banyumas

Wingko Babat

A traditional cake made from grated coconut and glutinous rice, originating from Babat and hugely popular in Semarang. A must-have souvenir to bring home.

From Semarang
